When Elon Musk first told the world that people would one day live on Mars, most experts simply laughed. Rockets, they insisted, were far too expensive to ever be reusable. But a small and stubborn team kept launching, kept failing, and kept learning from every single explosion. Slowly, the impossible started to look almost inevitable. Today, reusable rockets guide themselves back down and land upright on floating platforms in the middle of the ocean, and the cost of reaching orbit is a fraction of what it once was. The real lesson here is not about space at all. It is about what can happen when a determined group of people refuses to accept that a thing simply cannot be done.

Doubt is not the opposite of belief. It is the quiet space where belief is tested and made honest. We are taught to fear uncertainty, to reach for the nearest certainty like a handrail in the dark. But every real idea you hold was once a question you were brave enough to leave open. To doubt well is not to drift into cynicism. It is to hold two thoughts at once and refuse the comfort of a premature answer. The mind that can sit with not knowing is the mind that can eventually understand. So the next time certainty offers its easy hand, pause. Ask what it is you actually know. In that hesitation, something truer begins.

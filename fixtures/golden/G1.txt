Every morning, the city wakes slowly. Streets that were silent fill with quiet purpose. A baker unlocks her door before sunrise, flour still on her hands from the night before. Commuters move like a river toward the station, each carrying a small private world. On the rooftops, pigeons scatter as the first train rumbles beneath them. And somewhere, in a narrow apartment above a laundromat, a young writer stares at a blank page, waiting for the day to hand her a first sentence. The city does not wait for anyone. It simply begins again, one footstep at a time, until the ordinary noise of the streets becomes a kind of music.

Have you ever poured yourself a glass of beet juice, believing it was one of the healthiest choices you could make?

For many people, it is. But after the age of 60, pairing beet juice with the wrong foods could affect your kidneys, blood sugar, or even your blood pressure.

In this video, we'll look at four foods you should avoid combining with beet juice and three foods that make it a much healthier choice.

Beet juice is packed with natural nitrates, which your body converts into nitric oxide—a compound that helps relax blood vessels and support healthy circulation. That's why it's often recommended for heart health.

However, beet juice is also naturally high in oxalates and potassium while containing very little fiber. Depending on your health, these nutrients can become a concern, especially for people with kidney stones, diabetes, kidney disease, or certain blood pressure conditions.

The good news is that beet juice isn't the problem—it's how you pair it. Let's start with the first food you should be careful with.

Most small businesses fail not because their product is bad, but because they run out of cash. In the first year alone, nearly forty percent of new companies burn through their savings before they ever find steady customers. The founders who survive tend to share one quiet habit: they watch their numbers every single week. They know exactly how much money comes in, how much goes out, and how many weeks of runway they have left. When a slow month arrives, they cut costs early instead of simply hoping things improve. Growth feels exciting, but discipline is what keeps the lights on. A boring spreadsheet, updated on time, has saved more companies than any brilliant idea ever will.
